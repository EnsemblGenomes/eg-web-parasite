About Diphyllobothrium latum

The cestode Diphyllobothrium latum, or broad fish tapeworm, is parasite of fish and mammals which can cause Diphyllobothriasis in humans through consumption of raw or undercooked fish. Symptoms of diphyllobothriasis are generally mild, and can include diarrhoea, abdominal pain, vomiting, weight loss, fatigue, constipation and discomfort.
