About Haemonchus contortus

The nematode Haemonchus contortus, or red stomach worm, wire worm or Barber's pole worm, is an animal endoparasite infecting wild and domesticated ruminants (including sheep and goats) worldwide. It is a very common parasite and one the most pathogenic nematodes of ruminants. Adult worms are attached to abomasal mucosa and feed on the blood. H. contortus is a close relative of the human hookworm species which makes it an important model of parasitic nematode biology that is commonly used for experimental studies.

Annotation

Final gene prediction (v2.0) was performed by Augustus [PMID:16845043] using H. contortus specific parameters and RNA-seq, EST and polyA mappings as evidence hints generated by TopHat2 and PASA [PMID:18190707], respectively.

Assembly

The Pathogen Genomics group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute produced the genome data from a mixture of Roche454SLX and Illumina HiSeq sequencing technologies. The reads from each technology were initially assembled independently using assembly algorithms most suited for each one, and them merged using GAA version 1.0 [PMID:22025481].
