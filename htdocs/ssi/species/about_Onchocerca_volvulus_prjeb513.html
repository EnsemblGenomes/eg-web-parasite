About Onchocerca volvulus

The nematode Onchocerca volvulus is a gonochoristic filarial parasite of humans causing Onchocerciasis. Onchocerciasis, or River Blindness, is a neglected tropical disease (NTD) which affects over 37 million people, mainly in Africa. It is a severely debilitating disease, which is transmitted to humans by the bite of Simulium blackflies that live and breed near fast-flowing streams and rivers.

Annotation

The Parasite genomics group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute have collaborated with WormBase to produce first pass annotation of the genome, using MAKER2 [PMID:22192575]. WormBase has taken on responsibility for the manual curation of the gene set, and we welcome feedback from the Onchocerca community in order to help identify and prioritise curation targets.

Assembly

The reference genome for Onchocerca volvulus has been produced by the Parasite Genomics team at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. A combination of deep sequencing and optical mapping was used on material from a single adult worm from the Republic of Cameroon.
