About Bursaphelenchus xylophilus

Bursaphelenchus xylophilus is a gonochoristic species of the Clade IV nematodes. It is a parasite of Pine trees thought to originate from North America, but has spread through East Asia and Portugal. It is also known as Pine Wilt Nematode (PWN).

Annotation

EVidenceModeler [PMID:18190707] was used to combine all predictions from gene predictors Augustus [PMID:16845043], SNAP [PMID:15144565] and GeneMark.hmm [PMID:9461475], EST mapping data from PASA [PMID:18190707] and protein homology data against the Pfam database using GeneWise2 [PMID:15123596].

Assembly

The genome was sequenced by a collaboration of The Pathogen Genomics group at the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute and FFPRI Japan. Sequence data from 454 FLX and Illumina GAI were assembled using the Newbler de novo assembler (Roche 454 Life Sciences) and Velvet assembler [PMID:18349386] respectively.
