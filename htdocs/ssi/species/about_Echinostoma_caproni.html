About Echinostoma caproni

The trematode Echinostoma caproni, is a parasitic intestinal fluke that is widely used as a laboratory model. Geographic distribution includes South East Asia and the Far East, in countries including China, Korea, Taiwan, Philippines, Malaysia, Indonesia and India. Humans can become infected with Echinostoma by eating infected raw or undercooked food, particularly fish, molluscs, crustaceans and amphibians and symptoms can include abdominal pain, diarrhoea, tiredness and weight loss.
