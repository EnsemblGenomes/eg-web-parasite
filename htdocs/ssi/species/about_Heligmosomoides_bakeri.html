About Heligmosomoides polygyrus

The nematode Heligmosomoides polygyrus (also known as Nematospiroides dubius and Heligmosomoides polygyrus bakeri, and referred to by the NCBI taxonomy database and some authors as Heligmosomoides bakeri) is a common parasite found in the duodenum and small intestine of woodmice and other rodents. This organism is often used to model human helminth infection as it can establish chronic infection in different strains of mice.
